India has 535 million livestock animals — and less than 12% are formally identified.
Every year, the government loses thousands of crores to ghost animals,
duplicate insurance claims, and fraudulent subsidy disbursements.

Pashu Aadhaar AI solves this by giving every animal a permanent biometric identity —
just like Aadhaar for humans — using AI-powered face and muzzle recognition.

For rural India, this is transformational:
a farmer can now prove ownership of his cattle for a bank loan,
an insurer can verify the animal actually exists before paying a claim,
and the government gets real-time fraud detection before money leaves the treasury.

The AI here is not cosmetic — it runs a Siamese neural network on-device,
generating a unique biometric fingerprint for every animal in seconds.
No internet needed. No special hardware. Just a phone camera.

Let me show you how it works.
